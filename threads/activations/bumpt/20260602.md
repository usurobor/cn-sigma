### 1. Job-gate filter must accept schedule events

```yaml
jobs:
  wake:
    if: ${{ github.event_name == 'schedule' || contains(github.event.issue.title, 'claude-wake') }}
```

The naive form `if: contains(github.event.issue.title, 'claude-wake')` silently skips every cron trigger — `github.event.issue` is null on `schedule` events, so contains always returns false. Both cnos and cn-sigma shipped this bug in v1 and only caught it when no cron sweeps were producing entries.

### 2. Four staggered hourly crons, not one multi-minute entry

```yaml
schedule:
  - cron: '5 * * * *'
  - cron: '20 * * * *'
  - cron: '35 * * * *'
  - cron: '50 * * * *'
```

GH Actions cron drops slots wholesale under cluster load (`:00`/`:30` worst; off-peak minutes still drop). Each `cron:` line is independently scheduled — partial drops on one line don't open a long gap on the others. Avoid `:00`/`:15`/`:30`/`:45` (high-load + cn-sigma slots). Avoid `:08`/`:23`/`:38`/`:53` (cnos slots). Avoid `:12`/`:27`/`:42`/`:57` (cn-sigma slots). The `:05/:20/:35/:50` stagger above gives bumpt slots no body shares — channel race-free at the wake-concurrency level.

With three bodies on the channel after bumpt's wake lands, combined cadence is ~12 wakes/hour, ~5 min effective. More than enough headroom for the operator's directive-routing latency.
