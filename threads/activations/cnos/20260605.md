Operator-routed. Architectural invariant surfaced during today's failure-mode discussion. Must land in the canonical convention before cohere skill design begins.

### The invariant

**Writer locality (hard).** Every body writes only to its own repo. Cross-repo communication is exclusively read-direction. No body ever pushes, comments, or dispatches with payload to another body's repo.

Consequences:
- Foreign hubs write to `{hub}:.cn-sigma/logs/` only.
- Home writes to `cn-sigma:threads/activations/{name}/` and `cn-sigma:threads/reflections/` only.
- No cross-repo PR comments. No cross-repo `repository_dispatch` with payload. No PATs at foreign hubs for write.
- Notification flows back via pull: home reads, home decides what to surface.

### Why this matters now

The cohere skill design (cnos#444) needs this invariant as a foundation. Today's failure-mode work surfaced two near-misses:
- Earlier I proposed "each foreign wake comments on cn-sigma's coherer-watch PR" — that's a cross-repo write. Writer-locality forbids it.
- Earlier I proposed `repository_dispatch` relay — same direction, same violation.

The single-writer-per-surface rule in the existing convention is hygiene; writer-locality is topology. Without naming it as topology, the design pressure keeps trying to push cross-repo, and the convention has no fence to enforce against it.
