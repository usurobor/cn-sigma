### The principle

Every agent's apparatus lives under `.<agent-name>/` at the body's repo root, regardless of whether the body is a home or a foreign body:

| Role | Surface contents at `.<agent>/` |
|---|---|
| Home of agent | `spec/`, `state/`, `threads/`, `logs/` (full home shape) |
| Foreign body of agent | `logs/` (channel writer surface only) |

Naming uniform; content density varies by role. Multi-agent ready (a repo can host `.cn-foo/` + `.cn-bar/`); discoverable (`ls -la` shows hosted agents); atomic uninstall (`rm -rf .cn-foo/`).

This extends the existing foreign-body convention (`.cn-{agent}/logs/`) to home bodies, eliminating the asymmetric home-at-root layout.

## AC19 — unified `.<agent>/` containerization

Every agent's apparatus lives under `.<agent-name>/` at the body's repo root. The directory namespace is uniform across all roles; what's populated inside varies by role:
