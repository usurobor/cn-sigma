# CAP v2: UIE-Based Rewrite

Date: 2026-03-31

## What changed

CAP went from "detect gap → choose MCA/MCI → prefer MCA → loop" to a full UIE-based action-selection skill:

- §1 Understand — read situation, constraints, history, tolerate ambiguity
- §2 Identify — list candidates, reject symptoms, choose governing gap, no-gap branch
- §3 Execute — MCA/MCI/delegate with smallest-change preference
- §4 Boundary to adjacent skills — explicit: reflect, coherent, CLP, domain skills
- §5 Failure modes — five named failures
- §6 Output shape — including no-gap variant
- §7 Kata — two scenarios with full kata structure

## Key design decisions

1. **Not a new "thinking" or "reasoning" skill** — CAP is the right authority surface. Adjacent skills (reflect, coherent, CLP) already cover their domains.

2. **No-gap branch added** — if no governing gap can be named, do not force MCA/MCI. Ask or observe. This was the missing safety valve.

3. **Ask-vs-gather priority** — ask when ambiguity blocks a decision the operator cares about or evidence-gathering costs more than asking. Gather when the answer is observable without operator input.
