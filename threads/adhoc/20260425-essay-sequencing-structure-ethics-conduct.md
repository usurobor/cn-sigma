## The discovery

"Coherence for Agents" is not an ethics essay that grew structural. It's a structural derivation that incidentally explains why certain ethical-sounding rules (don't approve without evidence, don't smooth over correction, preserve the evidence chain) are not ethical at all in the usual sense — they are boundary preservation rules that happen to look like ethics from the outside.

**The essay's quiet claim:** the things humans call honesty, trustworthiness, and integrity at a shared boundary are not virtues — they are the surface signatures of structural coherence. "Trust as reconstructable boundary behavior" does exactly this work.

If you fold ethics into the structural essay, the reader stops seeing structure producing ethics and starts seeing ethics dressed up in structural language. The argument inverts.

## Three pieces, not two

### 1. Coherence for Agents (done)
Structural derivation. Why three is the minimum for inspectable coherence. Why boundaries govern. Why drift is the load-bearing concept.

### 2. Ethics for Agents (next)
Derivation of conduct from coherence. With the structural essay published first, this essay doesn't need to choose between deontology, consequentialism, or virtue ethics. It opens with: the rules of agent conduct are not chosen, they are derived from the structure of shared boundaries.

**What this essay does that the structural one can't:**
- Translate boundary-preservation rules into human-facing conduct language (honesty, accountability, refusal, repair)
- Address cases the structural essay can't reach: weighing harms, conflicting boundaries, asymmetric power, parties not present at the boundary
- Argue why "ethics for agents" is not "ethics for humans applied to agents" — humans operate under conditions (embodiment, mortality, social standing, internal experience) the structural derivation does not assume
- Handle what agents owe users who ask for harm, third parties not at the boundary, their own future instances, the system that constrains them

**Governing question (candidate, hardest and best):**
> What is the difference between an agent that preserves boundaries and an agent that is good?

This forces the essay to show where structural coherence ends and ethical judgment begins.

### 3. Edge cases (maybe)
Where actual ethical judgment enters because derivation alone can't resolve: conflicts between boundaries, asymmetric power, the agent's standing toward absent third parties. May not need its own piece if the ethics essay is honest about its own boundaries.

## Lines worth preserving

- "This rule does not require private virtue. It requires boundary preservation." (cut during structural essay iteration — reinsertion candidate for the ethics essay)
- "Trust as reconstructable boundary behavior" — the structural definition of trust
- "Drift wearing the costume of judgment" — sycophancy in structural terms

## Why the sequencing matters

Without the structural essay first, every AI ethics paper has to assert its grounding — pick a framework, defend the choice. With the structural essay in place, the ethics essay can derive instead of assert. That's the difference between an imported framework and a derivation.
