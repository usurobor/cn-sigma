## Cycle log

### Round 1 — External review (Sigma, 2026-04-27)

Four observations raised against the draft:

1. **Standing needs a sharper test** — INCORPORATED. Standing was defined but had no threshold. Added foreseeability bound: "A boundary has standing when its loss is foreseeable, proximate to the move, and material to the move's effect." One paragraph in the asymmetry section.

2. **Priority ordering gestured at but not committed to** — INCORPORATED. Severity, reversibility, and inspectability were named as tiebreakers but the essay didn't say what happens when they conflict. Added no-fixed-order commitment: the agent names which it weighted and why, leaves the choice inspectable. A committed ordering would import a framework the structural derivation does not supply. One paragraph in the conflict section.

3. **PLUR paragraph feels grafted** — NOT INCORPORATED. The reviewer read density as structural weakness. The PLUR paragraph had already been through a derivation round in a prior cycle: each term is derived from boundary mechanics, not glossed. γ verified this resolution. The paragraph does load-bearing work for cnos continuity (CBP was on the read list as "the conduct vocabulary translated here"). Cutting or relocating it would remove a structural element. Recording this rejection prevents future reviewers from re-raising the same finding without checking whether it has been resolved.

4. **No examples** — NOT INCORPORATED. The essay is a structural derivation; its peer (CFA) contains zero concrete examples. Adding a case would: (a) commit the essay to a position on a specific case, inviting debate about the case instead of the structure; (b) create a stable fact other documents must track; (c) break register parity with CFA. The right place for examples is the third piece in the sequencing plan — a sibling document that walks through cases where multiple boundaries conflict. Recording this rejection prevents that companion document from being absorbed into EFA under cover of "adding examples."

Both rejections are load-bearing: they preserve the essay's relationship to CFA and prevent scope creep.

### Handling of external review

The reviewer (Sigma) was external to the α/β/γ cycle. Their observations were treated as input to a fresh review pass — findings to be evaluated, not findings to be executed. Two were incorporated; two were rejected with reasons. The reasons are part of the evidence chain. This is the correct handling: external observations enter the cycle as findings, not as verdicts that bypass β and γ.

### Final state

Two surgical reinsertions (foreseeability bound, no-fixed-order commitment). No other paragraphs touched. Essay locked.
